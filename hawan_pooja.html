About Hawan Poojan

The Hawan Poojan at Vaishno Devi is one of the most revered and spiritually significant rituals performed at the holy shrine of Mata Vaishno Devi in Katra, Jammu and Kashmir. This sacred fire ritual, known for its deep spiritual and purifying effects, holds great significance for the devotees who embark on the pilgrimage to seek divine blessings from the Goddess. The Hawan Poojan symbolizes a connection between the material world and the divine through the medium of fire (Agni), which is considered a messenger that carries offerings and prayers to the gods. The ritual is an integral part of the worship and devotion experienced by millions of pilgrims visiting the Vaishno Devi temple. According to Hindu traditions, Hawan or Homa is a form of Vedic fire worship in which offerings such as ghee, grains, herbs, and other sacred items are poured into a consecrated fire while chanting mantras. The sacred fire symbolizes the Goddess’s divine energy, and through the process, devotees express their devotion, gratitude, and request for blessings. The flames of the fire are believed to purify the surroundings and the minds of the participants, cleansing them of negativity, impurities, and obstacles in life.
